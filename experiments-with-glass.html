Experiments with glass, light and found objects

A series of experiments using found objects, drawing on diverse, recycled materials and the interaction of light. The isolation faced by us all during the pandemic in 2020, lead me to feel like we were all lifeforms confined and thriving in consolidated spaces which hold our memories. The drawings within a fishbowl or a terrarium, symbolised a world containing us as creatures in our safe spaces. So stemming from the concept of living in a bubble literally came about these explorations of the Analog Terrarium.

The drawing on magnifying glass is a metaphor containing the memory of nature that has been observed and manipulated by mankind. It holds what once was in the land scape and is now threatened by ecological hazards, hence it lies encapsulated in a memory within the magnifying glass. When held against different landscapes it reflects an interaction of varied terrains synergising.
